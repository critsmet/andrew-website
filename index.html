You walk in.
You sit down.
You like the dark.
"A story’s just a story", you think. But you smile when you discover these stories happen to be real.
I tell stories,
piece the
world together. I'm bound to
voices like
telephone wire.
When I dream,
my dreams crawl into a bag
and then I have them with me.
All day. Not just at night.
I call my dreams glitter
because they sparkle.
“If you wake up in the morning, and you can’t think of anything but singing first, then you’re supposed to be a singer, girl.” – Sister Mary Clarence, Sister Act 2: Back in the Habit, 1993
